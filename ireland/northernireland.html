Black Taxi Tour

Take a black taxi tour to learn about the history of Belfast. On the tour you drive around and stop at various historical sites.

One stop is the Belfast Peace Wall that separates Irish nationalists and unionists. Irish nationalists tend to be Catholics and identify as Irish, while unionists tend to be Protestants and identify as British.

Add your name to the peace wall and you might even find the names of some celebrities who have also signed the wall.

Titanic Belfast

Learn about how the Titanic was built at its original construction site.

Titanic Belfast enlightens visitors on how and why the Titanic was built. There is even an interactive ride showing some of the construction!

The exhibit also focuses on the history of the Titanic and its course after it left Belfast.

Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge

Take a trip to the north tip of Northern Ireland and walk across the Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge.

The walk is an adventurous one as you are crossing a thin bridge over a cliff. If you have a fear of heights you might want to sit this one out.

The bridge connects Carrick-a-Rede island to the mainland.

Giant's Causeway

While up in the north, also stop by the Giant's Causeway.

The Giant's Causeway is made up of giant columns of stone. The stones are mostly hexagonal and form stepping stones.

According to legend, the Giant's Causeway was built by a giant. However, it was actually the result of a volcanic eruption.

Dunluce Castle

Come check out the ruins of an old castle on a cliff.

The castle has a long and interesting history that can be learned about at the visitor center.

Dunluce Castle has been used Dracula's castle in some films!
